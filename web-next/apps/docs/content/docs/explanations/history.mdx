---
title: History
description: Where NativeLink came from and why it exists.
---

NativeLink was built by engineers who'd run remote-execution infrastructure
at scale and gotten tired of the existing options. The trade-offs in 2023
looked something like:

- Java-based servers with GC pauses you could feel in your build times.
- Go services that handled load fine until a hot key took down a shard.
- Proprietary clouds with no open-source option.

NativeLink is what you get when you start from the constraints and write the
whole stack in Rust.
